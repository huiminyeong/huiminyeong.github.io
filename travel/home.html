About Malaysia

Malaysia enjoys a pleasant climate with abundant sunshine, captivating natural environments such as jungles, caves, and underwater worlds. All seasons are suitable for tourism, with the period from May to September being particularly ideal.

Moutain Kinabalu, Sabah

A challenge not to be missed for nature enthusiasts and adventurers! Mount Kinabalu, the highest peak in Borneo and Malaysia, also the third highest island peak on Earth. It harbors rich tropical rainforest ecosystems, and climbing Mount Kinabalu requires a certain level of physical stamina and technical skill!

Tioman Island

A natural marine playground beloved by divers. In Tioman Island, as soon as you dip your head underwater, you're in a dive site, where you can encounter a rich diversity of marine species.

Langkawi Cable Car

SkyCab, it is one of the main attractions in Langkawi, Kedah, Malaysia. With a total length of 2.2 kilometers, the journey from the bottom to the top takes approximately 15 minutes! If you're not afraid of heights, come and experience it!

Outfit Guide

When traveling to Malaysia, where it's summer-like all year round, it's essential to pack 3-4 sets of summer outfits. Additionally, due to strong UV rays, sunglasses and sunscreen are must-haves. Carrying a lightweight long-sleeved shirt is also recommended; it can shield you from outdoor sunlight and indoor air conditioning. This dual-purpose item is essential. Since many regions in Malaysia still adhere to conservative local customs, it's advisable not to dress too revealingly in most public places. This is especially important when visiting temples and other religious sites; please remember to respect local customs.
